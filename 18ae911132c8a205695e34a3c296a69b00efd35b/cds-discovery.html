CDS Discovery Research

THE BACKGROUND

Creating a roadmap for CDS API, NPR’s collaborative journalism API, to enable journalism sharking between local Stations and NPR.

CDS is the technology that enables the distribution of content throughout the NPR Network. It powers the access and republishing of NPR’s and other Station’s journalism alongside each other on their respective websites and apps.

THE TEAM

I worked as the sole designer in a cross-functional team with a product manager, engineering manager, and a Station relationship manager subject matter expert.

THE METHODOLOGY & TOOLS

As the design lead, I crafted the workshop plans, participant recruitment, and facilitated all the workshop sessions. Workshops were facilitated on FigJam.

The Problem & Opportunity

The NPR Network is a collaborative journalism effort between NPR and the 250 member local Stations across the United States. The goal of the NPR Network is to create a unique national and local blend of journalism by enabling collaboration between a more national organization (NPR) and a more local organization (Stations). This national/local blend of journalism delivers quality and extremely relevant news to local communities.

CDS is NPR’s proprietary content delivery API for the NPR Network, meant to enable a seamless exchange of journalism across participating organizations.

In order to build a roadmap that achieves this goal, we had to better understand our customers. To better understand our customers, I designed a series of workshops to gather feedback across the NPR Network.

These workshops aimed to answer the following questions:

  1. What are organizations’ collaborative journalism goals?
  2. What kind of pain points these organizations encounter when trying to collaborate with one another?

CDS is a relatively new API, with NPR clients being the heaviest users and Stations slowly onboarding onto, publishing to, and reading from it. Consequently, not much was known about how CDS users behave, what their goals are, and how they wish to use CDS to achieve their goals. These workshops were many people’s first time giving customer feedback to us.

Workshops

I designed this discovery effort to include two types of sessions, divided by organization type:

1. Station

Development/implementation teams: NPR SWE teams for production/publishing tools, app, website, and NPR+

Stakeholders: newsroom, analytics, station relationship managers, research & archival teams

2. NPR

Developers: software engineers/web developers who integrate their Station tools with CDS

Digital Managers: those who think about and manage digital news strategy for the Station

I facilitated all sessions, and all sessions were held in FigJam. Sessions were divided into three sections:

  1. Individual ideation: participants were coming from a wide range of organizations, types of Stations, roles at their respective organization, and levels of experience/familiarity with CDS
  2. Voting: upvoting ideas of interest and agreement was done to help guide discussion
  3. Discussion: free forum for participants to raise additional comments and/or expand upon a sticky note idea; discussion tended to start with the most popularly upvoted sticky note

Affinity Maps

After all the sessions, I created compilations of the approximately 250 sticky notes gathered to common ideas within and across user groups.

With additional weighting from the voting and open discussions, four themes emerged.

Findings & Recommendations

With the affinity maps, two main findings and resulting recommendations became clear:

1. CDS has a steep learning curve

  • Make CDS easier to use, understand, and adopt by anyone in the NPR Network
  • Provide visibility, controls, and insights into CDS for collaboration and troubleshooting

2. Content in CDS is not used effectively

  • Make it easier to build different types of audience experiences with items in CDS
  • Align strategies and goals towards collaborative journalism

Results & Impact

Here are some statistics about engagement with CDS Con:

  • 17 workshops were held
  • 10 Member organizations were represented at our informational CDS Con sessions.
  • We amassed 248 sticky notes during our CDS discovery sessions.

Our findings clearly indicated an appetite for a UI as the front-end of CDS, and building this brand new tool became a priority item on the team roadmap.

This research caught the attention of department leadership across NPR because it was so comprehensive and had not been done before. This included several senior directors and VPs from content/newsroom, Member Partnership, and digital products. We also gave this presentation to local station leadership across the NPR Network. This helped us gather the momentum needed to create the space on the team roadmap to scope and build a brand new tool that addresses CDS pain points.
